Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud – best for Indian context


UPI fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India, especially among students and online users. In a typical case, a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media platform, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a known contact. The fraudster then tricks the victim into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by sending a fake payment request link.

For example, a student trying to sell an item online may receive a message from a buyer who claims to send money through UPI. Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a “collect request” and asks the victim to enter their UPI PIN to receive the payment. Once the victim enters the PIN, money is deducted from their account.

Such fraud mainly targets people who are unaware of how digital payments work. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes leakage of personal information.

This case study shows how important it is to understand digital payment systems and avoid sharing sensitive details. Awareness and cautious behavior can prevent such cybercrimes.
